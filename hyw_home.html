About

Greetings! My name is József [ˈjoːʒɛf].

I am a linguist with a PhD in Linguistics from the School of Philosophy, Psychology and Language Science at the University of Edinburgh.


My PhD thesis, Reviving Bú-gí: An Investigation of Language Policy and Language Management in Taiwan, examined how policies, digital infrastructures, and local practices shape the revitalization of Indigenous and minoritized languages in Taiwan. Using a mixed methodology, it consisted of three studies focusing on key areas of Language Policy and Planning (LPP): status planning (Taiwan’s shift from official monolingualism to multilingualism, introducing the concept of “Mandarin-inclusive”), language management (policy implementation across macro and micro levels), and corpus planning (using digital ethnography and digital discourse analysis to study the standardization of written forms and the role of digital tools).


I am based out of Edinburgh, Scotland, & Urbana, Illinois, USA.
